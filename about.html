LaDuke

I make things because leaving an idea trapped inside my head seems unnecessarily cruel. Studio LaDuke is where those ideas become books, paddles, drawings, music, objects, experiments, and occasionally very strange websites.

Where I’m From

I live and work in Ketchikan, Alaska, where rain, cedar, boats, mountains, music, and stories all seem to collect in the same corners.

My work is shaped by place, family, Northwest Coast art, lived experience, and the stubborn belief that damaged things can become meaningful without pretending they were never broken.

What I Make

Books, drawings, paddles, furniture, upholstery, prototypes, websites, songs, wallets, shoes, and anything else that refuses to stay an idea.

Some projects are polished. Some are prototypes. Some are held together by epoxy, curiosity, and unreasonable confidence.

Why Studio LaDuke Exists

This is not one narrow brand with one tidy category. It is a workshop for the whole pile.

Studio LaDuke exists to make room for serious work, strange humor, recovery, experimentation, craftsmanship, and whatever comes next.

“I am not waiting until I have everything figured out. Apparently, that would take forever.” Studio LaDuke operating principle

Currently Under Construction

I am finishing my first book, Everybody Deserves to Go Home, developing Bob’s World, learning web design, building physical projects, and figuring out what happens when all of those things are allowed to share one roof.
